About Layal

Layal Liverpool is a science journalist and prospective author based in Berlin, Germany. She is currently a locum reporter at Nature where she reports on the scientific community, including bias and inequality in science, in addition to covering European policy and climate change.

Layal's journalism has spanned diverse science topics, including technology, physics, the environment and health, with a particular focus on inequalities in science, health and medicine. Her writing has appeared in Nature, New Scientist, WIRED, the Guardian and elsewhere, and her first book (expected in 2024) will investigate the health harms of racism in society, science and medicine.

Before moving into journalism, Layal worked as a biomedical researcher at University College London and the University of Oxford in the UK. She has a PhD in virology and immunology from the University of Oxford. Her PhD research investigated how invading viruses are detected by the immune system.

Selected articles

Based on her writing, Layal was shortlisted for a 2021 British Journalism Award, three Medical Journalists' Association Awards in 2021 and 2022, and a 2021 Association of British Science Writers Award.
