About me

I am a science journalist with expertise in biomedical science, particularly virology and immunology. I write news, features and opinion articles about the latest scientific research, from technology and space, to health and the environment. My writing has appeared in New Scientist, Wired and the Guardian. Currently, I am a Trainee Digital Journalist at New Scientist, based in Berlin, Germany.

Before moving into journalism, I worked as a scientific researcher at University College London and the University of Oxford in the UK. My PhD research focused on investigating how invading viruses are detected by the body’s immune system.
